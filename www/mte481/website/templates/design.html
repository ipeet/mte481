Design Details

System Model

In the illustration of the system model pre-existing wheelchair components are highlighted in yellow, of-the-shelf components in blue and specially designed elements in green. The control PCB has the dual responsibility of interacting with the wheelchair

Mechanical Design

The mechanical design will consist of five parts that can be mounted on to a wide variety of wheel chairs easily. The desired effect is to make this modular and applicable to a wide variety of wheel chairs so that we do not alianate parts of the market.All pieces will be made out of aluminum for the demo however a plastic would be more suitable for full development so that it has more elasticity.

The mechanical design is made so that it can be fastened to the side of the chair using bolts. The central tray is hinged at one side so that the user can raise or lower the tray in order to allow the user to enter and exit the chair easily. The sensors are offset from the base of the chair slightly to avoid detecting wheels and edges but remain within a small distance of the chairs edge to not reduce mobility to drasticly

Software Design

Software will be built on the Robot Operating System (ROS), which is a distributed message passing system capable of running on Linux systems. ROS systems are organized into a network of simple nodes, which co-operate to produce complex aggregate behaviour.
